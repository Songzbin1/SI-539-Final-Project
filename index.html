James Harden Career Highlights

James Harden is one of the most dynamic offensive players of his generation. Known for elite scoring, creative passing, and his signature step-back three-pointer, Harden has built a career filled with unforgettable performances across multiple NBA teams.

From his rise as a young star in Oklahoma City to his peak years in Houston and later chapters in Brooklyn, Philadelphia, Los Angeles, and Cleveland, his journey reflects growth, adaptability, and lasting influence on the modern game.

This website explores the major stages of Harden's NBA career, highlighting key team transitions, memorable moments, and the skills that made him one of the league's most recognizable and impactful guards.

Updated for the 2025–26 season: Harden was traded to the Cleveland Cavaliers in February 2026.

Career Journey

From a promising young guard to an MVP and veteran star, James Harden's career has included major achievements, team changes, and constant evolution in his style of play. Each stage of his journey reflects a different version of his game, from developing talent to offensive superstar to experienced playmaker and leader.

2009–2012

Oklahoma City Thunder

Harden began his NBA career with the Thunder. As a young guard alongside Kevin Durant and Russell Westbrook, he developed into one of the league's top young talents and won the 2012 Sixth Man of the Year Award.

2012–2021

Houston Rockets

In Houston, Harden became a true superstar. He won the 2018 NBA MVP award, led the league in scoring, and turned his step-back three and isolation offense into defining features of modern basketball.

2021–2022

Brooklyn Nets

After leaving Houston, Harden joined the Nets and showed another side of his game. His passing, playmaking, and control of the offense became even more central during this period.

2022–2023

Philadelphia 76ers

With Philadelphia, Harden continued to evolve. He remained a dangerous scorer while focusing more on setting up teammates, especially through his strong chemistry with Joel Embiid.

2023–2026

Los Angeles Clippers

Harden brought veteran experience, scoring, and playmaking to the Clippers. Even late in his career, he continued to perform at a high level and remained one of the most skilled offensive guards in the league.

2026–Present

Cleveland Cavaliers

In February 2026, Harden was traded to the Cleveland Cavaliers, beginning a new chapter in his career. His veteran scoring and playmaking now support a competitive Cavaliers team with championship ambitions.

From rising young talent in Oklahoma City to MVP in Houston and a veteran star in Cleveland, James Harden's career has been defined by skill, adaptability, and unforgettable moments.
